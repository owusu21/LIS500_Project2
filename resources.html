Implicit bias shapes our perceptions and decisions in ways we may not consciously realize, often reinforcing societal patterns of privilege and discrimination. The Implicit Bias Test (IBT) helps uncover these hidden associations, sometimes revealing biases that contradict our conscious beliefs. Intersectionality further expands this understanding by showing how overlapping identities—such as race, gender, class, and ability—intersect to create unique experiences of advantage or oppression. Recognizing these complexities allows us to challenge implicit biases and address the structural inequalities that shape our world. This page provides valuable resources on implicit bias, including research articles, videos, and tools to help understand and mitigate unconscious biases.
